Chicken Nihari

Chicken Nihari is a traditional and iconic dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent and is cherished for its rich, aromatic flavors. It is a slow-cooked stew that showcases the art of spice blending and tenderizing meat. The star ingredient, chicken, is simmered for hours in a tantalizing combination of spices, including cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and nutmeg, along with ginger, garlic, and a variety of ground spices. This slow cooking process allows the flavors to infuse deeply into the chicken, resulting in succulent, melt-in-your-mouth meat. The dish is characterized by its velvety, thick gravy, which is achieved by adding bone marrow to the cooking process. Garnished with fresh ginger, green chilies, and aromatic herbs, Chicken Nihari is traditionally enjoyed with naan bread or steamed rice, providing a hearty and satisfying culinary experience that is deeply rooted in the region's culinary heritage.

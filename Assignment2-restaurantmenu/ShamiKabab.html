Shami Kabab

Shami kabab is a popular Pakistani and Indian dish known for its soft and melt-in-your-mouth texture. It is made from a mixture of ground meat (usually beef or mutton) and lentils, along with a blend of spices and herbs. To prepare shami kabab, boneless meat is cooked with lentils (usually chana dal or split Bengal gram lentils) until they are tender. The cooked meat and lentils are then finely minced or ground together. To this mixture, a variety of spices such as ginger, garlic, red chili powder, cumin, coriander, and garam masala are added, along with fresh herbs like coriander leaves and mint. Some recipes may also include chopped onions or gram flour (besan) as a binding agent.

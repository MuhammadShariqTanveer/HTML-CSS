Chapli Kabab

Chapli kabab is a popular Pakistani dish that originated in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province. It is a flavorful and aromatic kabab (grilled or pan-fried patty) made with a mixture of ground meat, spices, and herbs. To prepare chapli kabab, a combination of minced meat, typically beef or lamb, is mixed with finely chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies, coriander leaves, and a variety of spices such as cumin, coriander, chili powder, turmeric, and garam masala. The mixture is then combined with a small amount of crushed or roasted gram flour (besan) and beaten egg to bind it together.

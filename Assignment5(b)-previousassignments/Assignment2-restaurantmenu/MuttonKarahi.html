Mutton Karahi

Mutton Karahi is a popular and flavorful dish that holds a special place in Pakistani and North Indian cuisine. It is named after the cooking vessel, a karahi, which is a deep, rounded, and thick-bottomed pan. This dish is known for its vibrant red color and robust flavors. To prepare mutton karahi, succulent pieces of mutton are cooked in a karahi with a medley of aromatic spices, including cumin, coriander, turmeric, and red chili powder. The mutton is often marinated beforehand to enhance its tenderness and absorb the flavors of the spices. Freshly chopped tomatoes and onions are added to the karahi, along with ginger, garlic, and green chilies, which contribute to the dish's tanginess and heat.

Mutton Qeema

Mutton qeema, also known as minced mutton, is a flavorful and versatile dish popular in South Asian cuisine. It is made by finely chopping or grinding mutton (goat or lamb meat) into small pieces, creating a textured and tender base for the dish. The qeema is then cooked with a variety of aromatic spices and ingredients, such as onions, garlic, ginger, and a blend of ground spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder. This combination of spices adds depth and complexity to the dish, infusing the mutton with a rich and savory flavor. Mutton qeema can be prepared in various ways, including as a curry, kebabs, or as a filling for samosas or wraps. It is a versatile ingredient that can be enjoyed as a main course, side dish, or even as a breakfast option, often accompanied by rice, roti (Indian bread), or naan. Mutton qeema is a comforting and satisfying dish that showcases the delicious flavors of South Asian cuisine.

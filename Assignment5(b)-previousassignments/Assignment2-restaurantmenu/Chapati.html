Chapati

Chapati, also known as roti, is a staple flatbread in South Asian cuisines, particularly in India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. It is a simple and nutritious unleavened bread made from whole wheat flour, also known as atta, and water. To make chapati, the whole wheat flour is combined with water to form a dough. The dough is kneaded until it becomes smooth and elastic. Small portions of the dough are then divided and rolled into round, thin discs using a rolling pin. The rolled chapati is cooked on a hot griddle or tawa until it puffs up and develops golden-brown spots on both sides.

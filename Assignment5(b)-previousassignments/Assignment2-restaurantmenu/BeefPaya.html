Mutton Paya

Mutton paya, also known as goat trotters, is a traditional and hearty dish that is popular in South Asian and Middle Eastern cuisines. Paya refers to the feet or trotters of the goat or lamb, and this dish is made by slow-cooking the trotters in a flavorful broth until they become tender and the flavors meld together. To prepare mutton paya, the trotters are thoroughly cleaned and then cooked in a large pot with a mixture of aromatic spices, such as cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Onions, garlic, ginger, and tomatoes are added to enhance the flavor further. The trotters are simmered on low heat for several hours until the meat becomes tender and the bones release gelatin, giving the broth a rich and thick consistency. The dish is typically seasoned with additional spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and red chili powder to add depth of flavor.
